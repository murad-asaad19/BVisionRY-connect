# BVisionRY Connect — Product Overview

> **Find the people who move your work forward.**
> Curated professional discovery for founders, leaders, builders, and investors.

---

## 1. What it is

BVisionRY Connect is a **mobile-first professional networking app** built around a simple idea: the connections that actually change your trajectory are *intentional and mutual*, not the product of cold outreach or an endless follower feed.

Each member states **what they're looking for right now** (their goal) and **the roles they hold**. The app then surfaces a small, **daily, quality-ranked set of matches** and provides the rails to act on them — **warm introductions**, an **opportunities board**, **office-hours bookings**, in-app **chat** (text + voice), and lightweight **meeting** scheduling with feedback.

It is deliberately **invite-only and curated**: quality of the network is the product.

---

## 2. The problem

For operators in the startup ecosystem, the existing options are poor:

- **Open social networks** optimize for reach and content, not for *the right introduction*. Inboxes fill with irrelevant cold pitches.
- **Warm intros** — the highest-trust path — are bottlenecked on who you already know and on the social cost of "asking around."
- **Discovery is noisy**: you can't tell a verified seed investor from a spammer, or a serious co-founder search from idle browsing.
- **Intent is invisible**: someone may be *exactly* who you need this month, but there's no signal that they're hiring, raising, or open to advising *right now*.

BVisionRY Connect compresses "who should I talk to, and how do I reach them credibly?" into a daily, low-effort loop.

## 3. Who it's for

### Primary audience
Members of the startup / tech ecosystem who hold one or more of four roles (a member can hold several — "a founder who also angels at $25K is a real combination"):

| Role | Who they are | What they typically want |
|------|--------------|--------------------------|
| **Founder** | Company builders, often raising or hiring | Co-founders, early hires, capital, advisors, peers |
| **Leader** | Senior operators / execs (VP, Head of, C-level) | Talent, peers, advisory roles, board/advisor seats |
| **Builder** | Engineers, designers, PMs, technical ICs | Founding roles, fractional work, collaborators |
| **Investor** | Angels, scouts, seed-stage funds | Quality deal flow, founders in their thesis |

### Representative personas
- **Maya, repeat founder (seed):** raising pre-seed and hiring a founding designer — needs warm intros to designers and aligned investors.
- **Sam, senior product designer:** open to a founding-design seat or fractional work — wants to be discoverable to credible founders.
- **Priya, seed investor:** wants curated, on-thesis founders without an inbox of cold decks.
- **Marcus, growth leader:** between roles, open to advising — wants peer connections and advisor opportunities.

## 4. Value proposition

- **Signal over noise** — a *small daily set* of ranked matches, not an infinite feed.
- **Intent-first** — everyone publishes a current goal, so timing is explicit.
- **Warm by default** — introductions (including **second-degree / mutual-connection** intros) carry trust that cold outreach can't.
- **Credibility you can see** — role-appropriate **verification badges** (GitHub, custom-domain email, Crunchbase, portfolio).
- **Curated & safe** — invite-only access, age + consent gating, blocking, reporting, and rate caps keep the network high-trust.

---

## 5. Core use cases

1. **Get matched daily** — receive a handful of ranked people aligned to your goal and roles; request an intro to the ones that fit.
2. **Send a warm introduction** — request an intro (optionally via a mutual connection); the recipient accepts or silently declines. Accepted intros open a chat.
3. **Hire / get hired** — post or browse the **opportunities board**; express interest; the author sees interested members and can send an intro.
4. **Raise / invest** — founders signal fundraising; investors signal they're writing checks; matching surfaces the overlap.
5. **Find a co-founder or collaborator** — co-founder and collaboration opportunity types plus role-aware matching.
6. **Get / give advice** — advising and "seeking advisor" intents, plus **office hours**: hosts publish bookable time slots that others reserve directly from their profile.
7. **Take a meeting** — propose a meeting in chat, confirm a time, get reminders, and leave a quick post-meeting review (used to keep match quality honest).
8. **Build a credible presence** — a structured profile with headline, bio, role details, verification badges, and a shareable public page.

### The opportunities board — eight intent types
`Hiring` · `Seeking role` · `Fundraising` · `Investing` · `Co-founder` · `Advising` · `Seeking advisor` · `Collaboration`

## 6. How it works (member journey)

```
Invite / waitlist  →  Sign up (age + consent gate)  →  Onboarding
   goal ("what are you looking for right now?")  →  roles + primary role
   →  AI-assisted headline & bio  →  handle, photo, location
        ↓
   Home: today's ranked matches  ──►  Request intro  ──►  Accept/decline
        │                                                   ↓
   Network · Opportunities · Office hours              Chat (text/voice)
        │                                                   ↓
        └──────────────►  Propose & confirm a meeting  ──►  Post-meeting review
```

**Five primary surfaces** (bottom navigation): **Home** (daily matches) · **Network** (connections + discovery) · **Inbox** (intro requests + chats) · **Opportunities** · **Profile** (+ settings).

## 7. Feature overview

- **Onboarding** — goal-first wizard with role inference and AI-assisted bio drafting.
- **Discovery & matching** — daily ranked matches with human-readable "why you matched" reasons; search and browse with filters; activity signals ("active this week").
- **Warm intros** — request → accept/silent-decline → chat; second-degree intros via mutual connections; cooldowns to prevent spam.
- **Chat** — text and **voice notes** (with transcription), meeting proposals, read-receipt controls.
- **Meetings** — propose/confirm/reschedule, reminders, ICS, and aggregated post-meeting feedback that informs ranking.
- **Opportunities** — eight intent types, tags, remote flag, expiry, and an "interested" list for authors.
- **Office hours** — bookable availability windows, slot durations, buffers, and meeting-link templates.
- **Profile & verification** — structured per-role fields, completeness meter, verification badges, and a public shareable page.
- **Privacy & safety** — private mode, blocking, reporting, read-receipt toggle, data export, account deletion.
- **Settings** — account, notifications matrix (push/email/in-app per event), appearance (system/light/dark), language (EN/ES), legal, help.

---

## 8. What makes it different

- **Quality is the product** — invite-only + verification + a *small* daily feed, rather than scale-at-all-costs.
- **Right-now intent** — goals expire/refresh, so matches reflect *current* needs, not a stale bio.
- **Warm-intro native** — the highest-trust connection path is the default action, not an afterthought.
- **Two-sided by design** — the same primitives serve "offering" (hiring, investing, advising) and "seeking" (roles, capital, advice), with a categorical taxonomy so intent is unambiguous at a glance.

---

## 9. Trust, safety & compliance

- **Invite-only** access with a waitlist; members can share limited-use invite codes.
- **18+ age gate** and explicit **Terms of Service / Privacy Policy consent**, recorded server-side before access is granted.
- **Verification badges** per role (GitHub commit, custom-domain email, `/team` page, Crunchbase, portfolio listings).
- **Abuse prevention** — blocking, reporting, sender-rate caps, intro cooldowns, and an account **suspension + appeal** flow handled by trust & safety.
- **Data rights** — in-app **data export** and permanent **account deletion**; analytics/crash reporting are consent-gated.

---

## 10. Platform & architecture (at a glance)

- **Client:** Flutter — one codebase for **iOS, Android, and Web**; Riverpod + go_router; light/dark themes; English + Spanish.
- **Backend:** Supabase (Postgres with Row-Level Security, SECURITY DEFINER RPCs, Realtime, Storage) plus Deno **Edge Functions** for push, voice transcription, account deletion, reminders, meeting playbooks, and goal-type inference.
- **Infra:** Firebase (push/analytics/crash) and Sentry, both consent-gated.

## 11. Status & open questions

- **Status:** pre-launch / launch-readiness — client is feature-complete across the surfaces above; backend ships schema, RLS, RPCs, and Edge Functions.
- **Monetization:** intentionally **open**. The anti-abuse rate-limit design assumes a future **Pro tier** could exist, but the model (subscription vs. other) is not yet decided.
- **Per-role structured fields:** an evolving area — additional structured profile fields per role are expected to deepen match quality over time.
